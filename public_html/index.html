Blessings, and Thank you for visiting The Psychic Ministry.

I believe that everything happens for a reason, and Spirit has brought you to this place to tell you that you are beloved, and God, the Angels, the Saints, and Spirit want to help you manifest your fondest dreams and turn them into reality. They are always there for you, but like a loving friend, they will not step in to help unless they are asked (that would be meddling). The good news is that when God, the Saints and the Angels are petitioned with proper prayer and intent, miracles can happen.

I should know. I have seen the miraculous my entire life, and I founded The Psychic Ministry and The Chapel of St. Dymphna Independent Spiritualist Church as an offering to God, the Saints and Angels for their miraculous intercession in a very tragic situation. I am caregiver to a loved one who has mental illness. My patient grew steadily more delusional over the years and repeatedly refused psychiatric treatment. After receiving contact from some people that my patient was afraid of, he decided that he must disappear to protect the rest of our family, which he did, without a trace. To say that I was horrified was an understatement; my loved one was not functional in any way, shape or form, and I was very concerned that he would commit suicide or get himself killed. I also had experienced a psychic dream about two months before, where a woman in a long dress (I know now that this was St. Dymphna) took me to psychiatric hospital and beckoned me to look into a window into a room. My loved one was inside, looking completely disconnected with reality, but I didn’t get a bad feeling about the whole thing. More like relief. When I woke up, I remembered the dream and wondered how that would ever happen, and pretty much forgot about it. I knew that it meant something, as I regularly have psychic dreams that come to pass, but my patient was so frighteningly resistant to conventional medical treatment that I was at my wits end.

Truly, “Nothing is impossible with God.” (Luke 1:37)

At this point, I realized that I needed supernatural intervention, and I began to petition St. Dymphna, who is the patron saint of runaways, psychiatrists, anxious, depressed or mentally ill patients and their caregivers. With special prayers, written petitions, proper candle burning, and focused intent, I prayed like never before.

Within five days, St. Dymphna worked the biggest miracle that I have ever witnessed, and it will stand as the most astonishing time of my life. I will never forget it.

Five days after I began my petitions, I got a phone call from a police officer. He had been called to a church by a minister, in a town two hours away. My loved one had come forward during the service and asked for an exorcism. Luckily, the church goers realized that he was in a psychotic state, and phoned the police, who had him involuntarily committed to a psychiatric hospital. He stayed there for a month, where he got a diagnosis and proper medication. As the days went by, it was like he was coming out of a fog, and he became balanced and lucid. It was truly a miracle.

St. Dymphna had answered my prayers. She had placed my loved one with people of God, who recognized his distress, and this led him to the miraculous healing that he has experienced with the help of a fantastic doctor and the proper medication.

My loved one is now a functioning member of our family. He knows the importance of taking his medication and keeping his thoughts uplifted and positive. It has truly been a miracle in our lives, and I hope to spread the word through this ministry to help others turn their dreams into reality…you just need to know how to ask! Never give up! The good that you desire in your life also desires you!! The word “de sire” is latin for “of the Father”, and I have been taught that our desires are put within us by God,as he knows that we can achieve them, and he wants us to have the highest and the best in our lives, as children of a loving Father.
